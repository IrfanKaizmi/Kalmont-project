Clothing Choices and Layering

Providing tips on selecting appropriate clothing is crucial. Our recommendations guide individuals on choosing insulated, moisture-resistant layers to retain body heat and protect against harsh cold.

Base Layer

Opt for moisture-wicking synthetic or wool base layers to efficiently draw sweat away, keeping the body dry.

Avoid cotton, as it retains moisture and may lead to dampness.

Mid-Layer (Insulation)

Choose insulating mid-layers such as thermal sweaters or down jackets for optimal warmth.

Consider mid-layers with breathability to regulate body temperature.

Outer Layer (Windproof and Waterproof)

CSelect outerwear with windproof and waterproof features to resist low temperatures, rain, and snow.

Prioritize adjustable outer layers to adapt to changing weather conditions.

Head Protection

Wear a warm hat or headband to cover the head, reducing heat loss.

Choose materials that are water-resistant and insulating, especially in snowy or rainy conditions.

Hand and Foot Protection

Use thick, insulated gloves with waterproof capabilities to prevent hand cooling and wetness.

Wear warm and waterproof boots to shield the feet from extreme cold weather.

Frostbite Prevention Recommendations

Emphasizing frostbite prevention is integral to our safety guidelines. The following are some suggestions designed to protect vulnerable areas, such as the face, hands, and feet, from extreme cold by using suitable protective gear:

<....../....>
Physical Protection

Use sunglasses or goggles to prevent snow blindness caused by sunlight reflection.

Regularly check the condition of hands to ensure the effectiveness of warmth-preserving measures.

Ensure boots fit properly, neither too tight nor too loose, to guarantee effective protection and insulation for the feet.

Utilize Portable Warmers

In extremely cold environments, consider using portable warmers, especially during prolonged stays, to provide additional warmth.

Keep Dry

Avoid wearing damp clothing and gear, as moisture may lead to faster body heat loss.

Periodically change into dry clothing to ensure both the body and garments stay dry.
